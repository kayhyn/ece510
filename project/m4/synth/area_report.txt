Dominant contributors
---------------------
1. The 128 inferred 64-entry x 8-bit per-lane weight register banks.
2. The 128 signed 8x8 multipliers.
3. Per-lane product, carry-save sum/carry, and result registers.
4. Clock-tree and timing-repair buffering across the full wrapper.

The RTL weight capacity is exactly 128 x 64 x 8 = 65,536 stored bits. Because
OpenLane/Yosys did not map these inferred banks to SRAM macros, those bits
require at least 65,536 sequential cells: **77.17% of the final design's
84,927 sequential cells**. This lower-bound count quantitatively establishes
weight storage as the dominant state contributor even before its read muxes
and write decode are counted. The 17,274 inserted hold buffers alone account
for 3.09% of all post-CTS cells; all timing-repair buffers together account
for 70,646 cells, or 12.64% of the total.

The available reports do not provide reliable hierarchical area after
flattening, so no unsupported module-level area split is claimed. Weight
storage is the main reason the final synthesized configuration uses L_MAX=64
rather than the full 576-element reduction depth. A practical next
implementation would replace inferred flip-flop banks with SRAM macros.
